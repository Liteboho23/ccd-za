CCD Participates in IEC Multi-Stakeholder Seminar on Electronic Voting

The Centre for Citizenship and Democracy (CCD) attended the IEC’s seminar on electronic voting in Pretoria. The event brought together key stakeholders, experts, and interest groups. CCD’s position is that, given current infrastructural, financial, and social challenges, e-voting is not feasible now.

Our focus remains on strengthening existing electoral processes, improving accessibility, digital literacy, and building public trust. Any move toward e-voting should be phased carefully, with significant investments in infrastructure, security, and legal frameworks, and only after foundational issues are resolved.

Programme Youth Engagement

Empowering youth through active citizenship

Empowering youth through active citizenship involves equipping young people with the knowledge, skills, and opportunities to participate in civic activities and contribute to their communities.

By empowering youth through active citizenship, we can foster a sense of ownership and responsibility among young people, helping them become engaged and informed citizens who can shape the future of their communities. This, in turn, can lead to more inclusive, equitable, and sustainable societies.

Partnership Schools

Partnering with schools for civic education

This enables CCD to instill the values of democracy, active citizenship, and social responsibility from an early age. By collaborating with educators, CCD delivers tailored programmes, interactive workshops, and engaging activities that equip learners with the knowledge, critical thinking skills, and confidence to participate meaningfully in their communities.

These partnerships ensure that young people not only understand their rights and responsibilities but also feel inspired to become active contributors to a just and democratic society.
